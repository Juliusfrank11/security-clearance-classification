Applicant  admitted  to  the  allegations  in  SOR  ¶¶  1.a.~1.d.,  and  1.f.  He  denied SOR allegation ¶ 1.e. After a thorough and careful review of the pleadings, exhibits, and testimony, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  43-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.  He  has  been employed with the defense contractor since 2007.  Appellant served in the U.S. Army for five years, achieving the rank of Staff Sergeant, and was deployed to Iraq.  He has held a security clearance since 2002, since joining the Army  after  “9/11.”    He  is  married  to  a  naturalized  American  citizen,  and  has  three native-born American children.  Guideline B - Foreign Influence 1.a.  and  1.b.  Applicant’s  mother-in-law  and  father-in-law  are  citizens  of  China, but reside in a retirement home in the United States.  They have “Green Card[s],” and are seeking to become U.S. citizens.  All they know about Applicant’s work is that he is “an  Engineer.”    They  never  worked  for  the Chinese government, nor were members of the Chinese Communist Party.  1.c.  Applicant’s  brother-in-law  is  a  citizen  and  resident  of  China.  He  is  an “Engineer,” working for a “power company.”  Applicant has little contact with his brother-in-law, perhaps “once a year . . . during the holidays.”  1.d.  Applicant’s  70-year-old  aunt  is  a  citizen  and  resident  of  China.  He  last contacted her about “15/20 years ago.”  1.e. Applicant is unsure if he has a bank account in China. He visited China in 2000, and deposited about $3,500 in a bank account to cover his travel expenses.   His  net  worth  in  the  United  States  is  about $2,500,000.  1.f.  Applicant’s  wife  once  had  a  company  that  partnered  with  a  Chinese company. That partnership ceased in 2012, about seven years ago.
