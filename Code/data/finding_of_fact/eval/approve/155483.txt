Applicant denied all four SOR allegations. After a thorough  and  careful review  of the pleadings, exhibits, and testimony, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  47-year-old  employee  of   a  defense  contractor.   He   has  been  employed  with  the   defense  contractor   since   2019.  He   does  not   hold   a security  clearance.    He  is married  to  a U.S.  citizen, and  has one  child, age  nine.  Applicant left  Iran in  1994.   He  first went to Turkey, then  to  Germany,  and  finally  to   the  United   States  in   2003.    He   later   received   a  bachelor’s  degree  from  an  American  university.    He   is  a   Christian;  and   as  such,   was  treated  like  a  “third class citizen” in Iran.  Guideline B - Foreign Influence 1.a.  Applicant’s  mother  is  a  citizen   and   resident  of  Iran.  She  is  “a  housewife.” Applicant  contacts  her  a  couple   of  times  a  year,  the  last  time  being  seven  to  eight months prior to his hearing.  1.b. Applicant’s father is a citizen and  resident  of Iran. He  is a retired legal clerk. Applicant has little  contact with his father, the last time  being three to  four years prior  to his hearing.  1.c.   Applicant’s  brother  is  a  citizen  and   resident  of  Iran.  “He  works  in   a  bank.” Applicant has little  contact with his brother,  the last time being about two years prior to his hearing.  1.d. Applicant’s mother-in-law and father-in-law are citizens and  residents of Iran. She  “used   to  work”   in   the  field   of  agriculture,  and   he  is  retired  from  a  company  that 2 made cement.  Applicant  has not contacted his in-laws in  four or five years.  Notice I  take  administrative  notice  of  the  following:  Iran  is  an  authoritarian  theocratic republic  with  a  Shia  Islamic  political  system.  Iran  has  been  designated  as  a  State Sponsor  of  Terrorism  since  1984.
