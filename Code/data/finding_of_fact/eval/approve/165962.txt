Applicant is a 35-year-old single man.  He served in the U.S. Navy from 2007 to 2011. His discharge was honorable.  He has a high school diploma and has taken some college classes.  He has been working for a defense contractor as an administrative assistant since September 2018.  Appellant incurred  approximately  $30,000  of  debt between  2010  and  2020.  He attributes most  of his  financial  problems  to  financial  mismanagement.   In  September 2018, Applicant took his current job  with  the  hope  that he  could begin getting  his debts under control.  Shortly  after  taking  the  job,  he  realized  that he  had  underestimated  the  cost of  living  where he  had  relocated  and  needed  more  income to  make  ends meet and  satisfy  his debts.   In  approximately  December 2018, Applicant was involved  in an  automobile  accident that totaled  his car.   Although  he  was not at fault, he  had  to  pay  out-of-pocket medical expenses while  his insurance  claim  was pending.  Consequently, Applicant could not make any debt reduction progress. In May 2019, Applicant negotiated a raise with his employer, which increased his salary from $60,000 per year to $75,000 per year.  After receiving his raise, Applicant began contacting his creditors and satisfying debts. Moreover, in an effort to increase his disposable income and facilitate debt elimination, Applicant recently resumed part-time work with two ride-sharing companies.
