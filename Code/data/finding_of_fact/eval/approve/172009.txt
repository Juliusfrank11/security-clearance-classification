Applicant is a 60-year-old, single man. In 2015, he earned an associate degree in engineering.  Since  2015,  he  has  worked  for  a  defense  contractor  as  an electrician.  In  2013, Applicant matriculated  to  college  to  earn an  associate  degree. He opened six  student-loan  accounts, as set forth  in subparagraphs 1.b  through  1.d, and  1.f  through 1.h  to  finance  the  education.  By  the  time  Applicant graduated  in 2015, he  had incurred  approximately  $40,000  of  student-loan  debt.    In  early  2019, Applicant missed   a  loan  payment.    The  lender then  contacted  Applicant,  offering  to arrange  a  weekly, wage  garnishment.  Applicant accepted  the  offer. Between  April 2019 and  February  2021, he  made  payments ranging  from  $148  and  $250.  In  March 2021,  Applicant  opted   to   end   the   garnishment  payments  and   entered   the   federal government’s  loan   rehabilitation   program.  Under  this  plan,  his  wages  are  no   longer garnished   and   he   now   pays  $561   per  month.    He  has  been   paying   without interruption since he  entered  this plan.   The  balance  of  these  student-loan debts  alleged  in subparagraphs 1.  through  1.d, and  1.f  through  1.h  totals approximately $26,000, and the debts are no longer in delinquent status.   Subparagraph 1.a, totaling $14,263 is a debt owed to a department store. In May 2020, Applicant contacted the creditor and negotiated an agreement whereupon he agreed to satisfy it through 11 monthly payments of $406 in full and final settlement.  He satisfied this account in August 2021.  Subparagraph 1.e, totals $1,712. Applicant satisfied this debt in February 2021.  Applicant satisfied subparagraph 1.i, totaling $453 in August 2021.  Applicant has no other commercial debt, other than his student-loan debts. He owns his home free and clear of any mortgages, and has no automobile-loan debt.
