Applicant  admitted  all   three   allegations  in   SOR.  After  a  thorough  and   careful review of the pleadings, exhibits, and testimony, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  42-year-old  employee  of   a  defense  contractor.      He   has  been  employed  with  the   defense  contractor since  1998, and has held a security clearance for about “20 of those years.”   He  is married, and has two  children.   Applicant  attributes the alleged  financial  difficulties to  his  wife  losing  her  job  as  a  Flight  Attendant  due   to  health  reasons   ,  and   his  tax   delinquencies  to  an  overwhelming  work  schedule  with  a  new position as Lead Engineer.    He  has a monthly, positive cash flow of over $1,500.  1.a. and  1.b. Applicant has filed his Federal  and  state income tax returns,  albeit in   an  untimely  fashion,  for   tax   years  2014  through  2017,  as  evidenced  by  tax documents.      Tax   years 2014 through 2016 were filed prior to the issuance  of the SOR. Tax  year 2017 was filed with an extension, and with his 2019 income tax return,  in April of 2020.  1.c.  Applicant is current with his home mortgage, as evidenced by documentation from the Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp.
