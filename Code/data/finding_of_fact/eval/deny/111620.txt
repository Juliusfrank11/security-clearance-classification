In Applicant’s SOR response, he admitted the SOR allegations in ¶¶ 1.d, 1.e, 2.a, and 2.b.  He denied the other SOR allegations. He also provided extenuating and mitigating information. His admissions are accepted as findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  34-year-old  avionics  equipment  specialist  working  for  a  defense contractor for the past two years.  In 2003, he graduated from high school, and he completed three years of college.  He served in the Air Force from 2004 to 2008.  When he was discharged from the Air Force, he was a senior airman, and he received an honorable discharge.  His Air Force specialty was nuclear cruise missile maintenance technician.  For most of his Air Force service, he had a top secret clearance with access to sensitive compartmented information .  He was married from 2006 to 2012, and he does not have any children.  Drug Involvement and Substance Misuse SOR ¶ 1.a alleges that from about 2003 until at least October 2014, Applicant used marijuana with varying frequency. SOR ¶ 1.b alleges that from about March 2005 until June  2008,  Applicant  used  marijuana  with  varying  frequency  while  holding  a  security clearance. SOR ¶ 1.c alleges that from about  March 2012 until at least October 2014, Applicant used marijuana with varying frequency while employed in a position of public trust.  Applicant  denied  SOR  ¶¶  1.a  through  1.c  explaining  that  his  marijuana  use  was limited to June and July 2014.  SOR ¶ 1.d alleges that from about June 2014 to July 2014, Applicant purchased marijuana with varying frequency. SOR ¶ 1.e alleges that in about May 2014, Applicant was  arrested  and  charged  with  possession  of  drug  paraphernalia  and  possession  of marijuana while operating a motor vehicle.  In May 2014, Applicant had a drink of alcohol before he started driving home.  A police officer said he crossed the 2 center line.  He also had an open container of alcohol in his vehicle.  The police officer searched Applicant and found marijuana in his possession.  He also had a small glass marijuana pipe in his vehicle.  He was not charged with an alcohol offense. When he went to court, he received deferred adjudication for one year,  and  his  record  was  expunged  after  one  year.    Applicant  said  he  used marijuana  three  or four  times  in  May  2014.    He  insisted  he  did  not  use marijuana before May 2014.  He clarified that he used marijuana 10 to 15 times or  once  or  twice  a  week  from  May  through  July  2014.    He  held  a  public  trust position when he possessed marijuana.  SOR  ¶  1.f  alleges  that  in  about  October  2014,  Applicant  was  diagnosed  by  a licensed addiction  counselor   with  a  condition  called  Cannabis  Abuse. Alcohol Consumption SOR ¶ 2.a alleges that Applicant was arrested in  about July 2014, and charged with  operating  a  vehicle  while  intoxicated.  Applicant  was  sad  or  depressed  about  his situation after his marijuana-related arrest in May 2014, and he made a bad decision to drink excessive amounts of alcohol.  In July 2014, he drank eight or nine beers over a six-hour period.  The police stopped Applicant’s vehicle for improper lane change and asked him if he drank any alcohol that day.  Applicant denied that he drank any alcohol to the police officer because he did not want to go to jail.  Applicant subsequently told the police officer that he only had two or three drinks.  The preliminary blood alcohol test indicated .129 BAC.  A police officer found an open container of beer in Applicant’s vehicle.  Upon arrival at jail, the police found marijuana on Applicant’s person.  Two breath tests were administered at the jail with results of .10 and .11 BACs.  Applicant was not charged with possession of the marijuana found on his person in July 2014.  Applicant pleaded guilty to operating while impaired , and he was  sentenced  to  six  months  of  probation,  to  pay  a  fine,  to  have  a  substance-abuse assessment, and to complete a 16-hour alcohol-treatment class.  Applicant did not inform his supervisor or security officer of his arrests in May and July 2014.  He said he did not inform the government of his arrests because of “fear of losing  my  job.”    About  a  year  after  his  second  arrest,  the  government conducted a  background  check of  contractor  employees  because of  a  security  breach unrelated to Applicant’s conduct, and his employer discovered his two arrests in 2014.  Applicant’s contractor employer fired him.  SOR ¶ 2.b alleges that Applicant was diagnosed by an LAC with a condition called Alcohol Abuse. Applicant consumed alcohol since the age of 17.  In 2014, he was drinking alcohol four or five times a week.  4 In Applicant’s September 17, 2018 response to DOHA interrogatories, he said he consumed two to three drinks, two to three times a month.  The most recent time he was intoxicated was on September 15, 2018.  Applicant stated at his hearing that he has dramatically reduced his alcohol consumption to about once every two months.  There is no evidence of alcohol-related incidents involving the police or the courts after July 2014.
