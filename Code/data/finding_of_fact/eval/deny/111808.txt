In  1983,  Applicant  graduated  from  high  school,  and  in  1987,  he  received  a bachelor’s degree with a major in electrical engineering.  In 1994, he received his first master’s degree in information systems; in 2002, he received his second master’s degree in military operational art and science; and in 2016, he received his third master’s degree  in  science  and  religion.    In  1998,  he  married  and  his  two  step children are ages 34 and 38.  Sexual Behavior, Use of Information Technology, and Personal Conduct SOR ¶¶ 2  and 3  cross-allege conduct that is alleged in SOR ¶ 1 . 2 SOR ¶ 1.c alleges Applicant received verbal counseling on at least two occasions for viewing pornographic websites from August 1998 to July 2001.  On about two or three occasions,  he  used his government-owned  desktop  computer in  his  Air  Force office to access pornographic websites for 10 to 15 minutes on each occasion.  It was at the end of the duty day.  He did not intentionally download any pornography on his computer.  He was verbally counseled and told to stop accessing  pornographic  websites  with  his  government  computer.    The  first counseling did not seem to be with serious intent.  “It was given to me with like a wink” with an instruction to “knock it off.”  The policy against using a government computer to access pornography was informal and not well developed at that time.  He acknowledged that his computer had access to sensitive information, and his actions  could  have  compromised  that  information  technology  system  by  exposing  it  to viruses and malware.  At the time he was accessing the pornographic sites, he was not thinking about the risk to sensitive information.  SOR ¶ 1.b alleges Applicant received a letter of reprimand in about October 2004 while serving on active duty for misuse of a government computer.  He admitted that he accessed pornographic websites on a government-issued computer.  The computer he used was a Morale, Welfare, and Recreation  computer that was in a break area.  The MWR computer was for general access to enable Air Force employees to access their email, and the MWR computer was not assigned to Applicant.  He  went  to  two  or three pornographic  websites  over the  course  of one day  in September 2004.  He did not intentionally download any pornography on the MWR computer.  His computer misuse was detected through a computer log that was  used  to  monitor  the  computer’s  use.    There  was  no  evidence  that  the pornography  was  downloaded  or  saved  on  the  MWR  computer.    In  2004,  he viewed  pornography  at  home  on  an  infrequent  basis.    He  received  some counseling from a licensed counselor to address his pornography issue; however, it was ineffective.  The letter of reprimand was for violation of a regulation under Article 92, Uniform Code of Military Justice . See. e.g., DOD Regulation 5500.7-R, Joint Ethics Regulation, , ¶ 2-301. The reprimand adversely affected his future assignments, and he was not selected for promotion to colonel.  During his November  7,  2007,  Office  of  Personnel  Management  personal  subject  interview, Applicant  said  he  learned  his  lesson  about  misuse  of  government  computers  after  his reprimand in 2004.  SOR  ¶  1.a  alleges  Applicant  used  an  employer-owned  computer  to  view pornographic websites from about January 2018 to about July 2018.
