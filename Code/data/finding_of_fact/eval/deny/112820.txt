Bangladesh’s country status Bangladesh  is a country located on the northern edge of the Bay of Bengal. See Background  Note:  Bangladesh  at  1,  U.S.  Department  of  State    It  is bordered  on  three  sides  by  India  and  shares  a  small  border  with  Burma.  It  has  a population  of  approximately  160  million  and  a  land  area  of  55,813  square  miles,  which approximates  the  size  of  the  state  of  Wisconsin.    Bangladesh  has  a  literacy rate  of over  60%  and  about  98%  of  the  population  are  ethnic  Bengali  and  speak  Banla.  Non- Urdu-speaking  non-Bengalis  of  Indian  origin,  and  various  tribal  groups  comprise  the remaining 10.5 % of the population.  Historical background Originally part of British India, Bangladesh became known initially as East Bengal following  the  partition  of  British  India  along  religious  lines  in  1947.    The predominantly  Muslim  eastern  half  of  the  region    was designated East Pakistan ; while the predominately Hindo western part became the Indian state of West Bengal.  Frictions developed soon after the partition between East and West Pakistan. East Pakistanis  felt exploited by the West Pakistan-dominated central  government.  Linguistic,  cultural,  and  ethnic  differences  contributed  to  the estrangement  of  East  from  West  Pakistan.  Following  failed  political  talks  in  1971. Pakistani  president  Yahya  Khan  postponed  indefinitely  the  pending  national  assembly session, precipitating massive civil disobedience in East Pakistan. The  crisis  in  East  Pakistan  created  new  strains  in  Pakistan’s  relations  with  India that  produced  warring  relations  in  1965  between  the  two  countries,  mainly  in  the  west.   The  new  tensions  prompted  India  to  intervene  on  the  side  of  the  Bangladeshis  in November 1971, and in December 1971 Pakistani forces surrendered, paving the way for the  birth  of  a  new  Bengal  country.  In  April  1971,  Bangladesh  became  an  independent country,  recognized  by  the  United  States,  and  formed  as  a  parliamentary  democracy under  a  1972  constitution.  See  A  Guide  to  the  United  States’  History  of  Recognition, 7 Diplomatic, and  Consular Relations, by Country, since 1776: Bangladesh,  U.S. Dept.  of State  Despite military coups following its independence in 1971, Bangladesh has made considerable progress toward a more prosperous and pluralistic society. The country was returned to democratic rule in December 2008 and is now a parliamentary republic. See CIA World Factbook: Bangladesh .
