In Applicant’s SOR response, he admitted SOR ¶¶ 1.a and 1.b.  He admitted that he failed to timely file his state and federal income tax returns for tax years 2014, 2015, 2016, and 2017.  He also provided extenuating and mitigating information. Applicant’s admissions are accepted as findings of fact. Additional findings of fact follow. Applicant is 54 years old, and he is an information technology specialist.  In 1984, he graduated from high school.  In 1991, he received a bachelor’s degree with a major in electronics engineering.  He is not married, and he does not have any children.  He has not served in the U.S. armed forces.  Applicant has  specialized  in  network  engineering  since  1996,  and  he  has  worked  on  various contracts for DOD and private entities.  Financial Considerations Applicant provided 11 reasons to explain why he failed to timely file his federal and state  tax  returns  for  tax  years  2014  through  2017:    his  nephew  had  three  brain surgeries; - his stepfather, informal stepfather, and sister’s fiancé all passed away from cancer;  his grandmother passed away;  his father had serious health issues;  his significant other’s sister had stage four breast cancer;  in 2014, his significant other had hip replacement surgery;   in 2018, his significant other suffered a serious head and foot injury;  in 2014, he ran for political office; and  he was busy running a  farm  and  business.    Applicant  also  had  some  difficulties collecting relevant financial and tax information, organizing it, and coordinating with his accountant.  He was the only employee of his information technology business.  He had a pile of documents in his office, and he did not know how to organize the documents to present them to his accountant.
