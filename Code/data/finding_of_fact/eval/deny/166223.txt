In Applicant’s SOR response, he admitted the SOR allegation in ¶ 1.a.  He also  provided  mitigating  information.  Applicant’s  admission  is  accepted  as  a  finding  of fact. Additional findings follow. Applicant  is  29  years  old,  and  he  has  worked  for  defense  contractors  as  a mechanical engineer or as an associate member staff engineer since December 2014.  In 2014, he was awarded a bachelor’s degree, and since 2016, he has been participating in a post-graduate education program.  He has not served  in  the  military.    He  is  not  married,  and  he  has  been  in  a  cohabitant relationship since 2018.  He does not have any children.  Drug Involvement and Substance Misuse SOR ¶ 1.a alleges in March 2019 Applicant used marijuana twice while granted access to classified information.  In his December 8, 2014 SCA, Applicant said he used marijuana about once a month during his first two years of college, and his most recent marijuana use was during Hurricane Sandy  .  He did not hold a security clearance when he used marijuana in 2012.  In his 2014 SCA, he said: I do  not  plan on using  this drug because  since  then  I  have  removed  myself from   that   environment   and   no   longer  feel  attracted   to   partaking   in  that behavior. I have  realized  that  some  of the  decisions I  have  made  in the  past can negatively affect my future and  my capacity to work.  On  March  31,  2015,  an  Office  of  Personnel  Management    investigator interviewed  Applicant.    His  2015  OPM  interview  was  consistent  with  his  2014 SCA. His only illegal drug use involved his use of marijuana.  The OPM summary of interview states, “[Applicant] has no intention of any future [illegal] drug use. He is not interested in future drug use, and he realizes that using drugs can negatively affect his future and his capacity to work.”  2 In his June 7, 2019 SCA, Applicant said that he used marijuana from November 2010 to October 2012 about once a month during his first two years in college.   He  did  not  use  marijuana  from  2012  until  March  2019.    He  used marijuana twice in March 2019 while he held a security clearance while “in a social setting after having a few drinks with some college friends who were visiting from out of state.”   He  does  not  continue  to  associate  with  the  college  friends  with  whom  he  used marijuana.  He said: I  do   not   intend   to   use   this  drug   in  the   future   because   I  consider  this  an isolated  instance  and  I  would like  to  note  that I am  not  a  habitual user by any means. I am  extremely remorseful of the  choice that I  made  and it was a complete lapse  of judgement.  On  August  21,  2019,  an  OPM  investigator  interviewed  Applicant.    His interview about Applicant’s marijuana use was consistent with his 2019 SCA.  He said he does not intend to use marijuana in the future, and he does not associate with the people who used marijuana with him in 2019.  He has not tested positive for use of illegal drugs.  His 2019 marijuana use was while he held a security clearance.  In his SOR response, Applicant described his 2019 marijuana use as “an isolated event,”  and  a  “lapse  of  judgement.”    He  observed  that  recreational  use  of marijuana  in  his  state is not  prohibited by state  law.   In  July 2020, he moved  to  a different state.  He promised to comply with laws, rules, and regulations, and not to use marijuana in the future.  In  his  FORM  response,  Applicant  expressed  remorse  for  his  marijuana  use  in 2019.  On September 17, 2021, he provided a hair sample for testing, and the results from a hair follicle drug screen indicating no use of illegal drugs.
