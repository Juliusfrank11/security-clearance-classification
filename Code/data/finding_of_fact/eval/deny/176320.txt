Applicant is a 58-year-old high-school graduate with two adult children. He has been married since 2005. A previous marriage ended in divorce in 1989. Since 2019, he has been working for a defense contractor as a custodian.  Applicant has approximately $57,445 of delinquent debt. As of the date of the SOR, approximately $49,000 of this debt constitutes a child support arrearage, as alleged in subparagraph 1.a.  Approximately $4,000 of this debt constitutes delinquent medical debt owed to the same hospital.  Applicant contends that he incurred his debts because he tried “to handle too many things at one time.”  He has been paying his child support payments through a wage garnishment.  As of December 2021, the balance was $46,445.  In February 2021, one month after the SOR issuance, Applicant consulted a debt relief agency to help him settle his debts.  With the help of the debt relief agency, he organized a payment plan. It includes debts that were not listed in the SOR, including an $8,829 hospital bill.  Under the program, which is expected to last 50 months, Applicant is to make monthly payments of $325.  He provided no documentation of any payment chronology.
