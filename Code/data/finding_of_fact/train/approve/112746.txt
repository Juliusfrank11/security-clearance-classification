Applicant  admitted  the  two  allegations  of  the  SOR,  ¶¶  1.a.,  and  1.b.  After  a thorough  and  careful  review  of  the  pleadings,  exhibits,  and  testimony,  I  make  the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  53-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.    He  has  been  employed  with  the  defense  contractor  since  August  of  2014.   He  is married,  and has  three adult  children.    Applicant  attributes  his  alleged  financial  difficulties  to  the  crash  of  the housing  market  in  2008,  and  to  a  year  of  unemployment  prior  to  his  present employment.  Guideline F - Financial Considerations 1.a Applicant admits a past-due debt for landscaping to Creditor A in the amount of about $33,000.  He has settled this debt for $15,000,  and  has  made  the  first  of  three  $5,000  payments  as  evidenced  by documentation from Creditor A.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 1.b. Applicant admits a past-due debt as the result of a foreclosure to Creditor B.  This  debt  was  satisfied  more  than  six  years  ago through the foreclosure, as evidenced by the Government’s July 2012 credit report.   This allegation is found for Applicant. Applicant’s  wife  corroborated  his  testimony,  and  his  father  and  brother  testified as to Applicant’s good character.
