Applicant  admitted  to  both  allegations  of  the  SOR,  ¶¶  1.a,  and  1.b.  After  a thorough  and  careful  review  of  the  pleadings,  exhibits,  and  testimony,  I  make  the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  39-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.  He  has  been employed  with  the  defense  contractor  “for  over  14  years.”   He is married, and attributes his alleged financial difficulties to his wife losing her job.  Guideline F - Financial Considerations 1.a. Applicant admitted a past-due student loan debt to Creditor A in the amount of about $20,388. He mistakenly thought this private student loan, along with his other student loans, was deferred.  This debt “has been  settled,”  with  a  current  “Balance:  $0.00,”  as  evidenced  by  correspondence  from Creditor A.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 1.b.  Applicant  admitted  past-due  auto  loan  debt  to  Creditor  B  in  the  amount  of about $10,013. He mistakenly though this debt was covered by his gap insurance when his vehicle was voluntarily repossessed.   This  debt  has  been  settled  for  $5,000,  as  evidenced  by correspondence from Creditor B.  Furthermore, this debt does not appear on Applicant’s most recent April 2019 credit reports.  This allegation is also found for Applicant.
