Applicant admitted to the allegations in  SOR ¶¶ 1.a.~1.d., and 1.g.. She denied SOR  allegations  ¶¶  1.e.,  1.f.,  1.h.,  and  1.i.. After a  thorough  and  careful review  of  the pleadings, exhibits, and testimony, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  39-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.   She is divorced from an abusive husband, and has three children, two of which live with Applicant and for whom she receives only “$70.00 a week” in child support.   Applicant  attributes  her  financial  difficulties  to leaving her abusive husband, coupled with periods of unemployment.  She currently has about $9,000 in a savings account, and about $1,000 in a checking account.  Guideline F - Financial Considerations 1.a.  Applicant  admits  that  she  owes  about  $11,659  on  a  past-due  credit  card account. Since October of 2018, Applicant has been making monthly payments of $250, as evidenced by a payment agreement with the creditor.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 1.b. and 1.c. As a result of her divorce, Applicant and her former spouse had an automobile and a motor cycle repossessed.  She  has  reached  a  settlement  agreement  with  the  creditor  to  pay  $3,079  towards  the auto  debt  ,  and  $4,443  towards  the  motor  cycle  debt  , with  payments  to  be made  “no  later  than  October  11,  2019,”  as  evidenced  by  correspondence  from  the creditor.  I find Applicant is making a good-faith effort to repay these debts. 1.d.    Applicant  admits  that  she  owes  about  $1,256  on  a  past-due  cell  phone account.  Since  September  of  2018,  Applicant  has  been  making  monthly  payments  of $125, as evidenced by receipts from the creditor.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 1.e. and 1.f. Applicant denies that she owes State E about $6,494.  Her inquiries to State E have come up empty, and no such debt appears on Applicant’s  November  2018,  March  2019,  and  April  2019  credit  reports  .   These allegations are found for Applicant. 1.g.  Applicant  admits  that  she  owed  the  Department  of  Defense    about $497 as the result of an overpayment by DoD.  This  alleged  past-due  debt  was  paid  in  September  of  2018,  as  evidenced  by  a “Payment Confirmation” by DoD.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 2 1.h. and 1.i. Applicant denies that she owes the Department of Veterans Affairs about $5,303. This debt was “paid” through the Department of Treasury, as reflected on Applicant’s  April  2019  CR.   These allegations are found for Applicant.
