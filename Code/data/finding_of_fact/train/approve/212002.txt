Financial Considerations SOR ¶ 1.c alleges Applicant filed a Chapter 7 bankruptcy in around June 2002. This  bankruptcy  was  discharged  in  around  February  2003.    Applicant  filed  for bankruptcy after his first wife lost her job.  His first spouse was unfaithful; she had health problems; and he divorced her in 2004.  This bankruptcy initially was a Chapter 13 bankruptcy, and he made about six payments before he converted it to a Chapter 7 bankruptcy.  He estimated that about $25,000 was discharged in the 2003 bankruptcy.  SOR ¶ 1.b alleges Applicant filed a Chapter 13 bankruptcy in around July 2007. Applicant said he was unable to make the monthly payments, and he elected to convert the Chapter 13 bankruptcy to a Chapter 7 bankruptcy around January 2009.  The bankruptcy  documentation  lists  five  secured  debts  totaling  about  $521,000,  which 2 includes four vehicle loans and a real estate loan for about $442,000.  He had 10 unsecured nonpriority debts totaling about $3,800.  The Chapter 7 discharge was denied in around July 2009. The  dot com crash resulted in  the   loss  of  employment  to   thousands  of  IT  workers,  including   Applicant.    Applicant was unemployed  for six months.
