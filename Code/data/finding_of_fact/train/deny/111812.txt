Applicant  is  a  51-year-old  quality  inspector  who  has  worked  for  a  defense contractor since June 2018 and seeks to obtain a security clearance. His current annual salary is approximately $110,000.  He married in 2007 and was divorced in May 2019.  He has two children ages 25 and 20 and two stepchildren ages 23 and 25.  He has no child support obligation.  He is currently living with his parents to save on expenses.  The SOR alleges a $72,574 student loan collection account ; a $3,509 telephone service collection account ; a $1,092 credit card collection debt ;  an  $833  bank  credit  card  collection  account  ;  and  a  $155  bank  card account  in  collection.  The  four  debts,  other  than the  student loan  obligation, total approximately $5,500. He has not made any payments on these four debts.  At  the  time  of  his  September  2018  enhanced  subject  interview,  he  had  no information about these debts and speculated that they might have been joint credit cards he shared with his wife. At the time of his subject interview he was arguing with his wife as to who was responsible for a telephone collection debt .  The debt became so large because his then wife had used the account to purchase a number of new cell phones.  He admitted all of the debts in the SOR except for a $155 collection debt .
