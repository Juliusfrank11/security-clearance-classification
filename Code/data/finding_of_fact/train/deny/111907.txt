Background Applicant is a naturalized U.S. citizen and a dual citizen of Israel who retains his Israeli  passport  that  was  issued  to  him  in  June  2003  and  was  renewed  in  February 2014.    His  Israeli  passport  is  not  scheduled  to  expire  before  February  2024.   Applicant  immigrated  to  the  United  States  in  October  1997  and  became  a naturalized  U.S.  citizen  in  April  2009.    He  was  issued  a  U.S.  passport  in  May 2009 and only uses his Israeli passport to gain ingress to Israel and egress to return to the United States.  Applicant married in October 1978 and has two children from this marriage, ages and 34 and 40.  Applicant  attended  a  Russian  university  between  1977  and  1981,  but  did  not earn a degree before immigrating to Israel in 1981. Between 1982 and 1985, he studied engineering at an Israeli university, but did not earn a degree.  Whether he has resumed  his  studies  in  Israel  or the  United States  with  the  objective  of  completing his degree requirements is unclear. Since  February  2018,  Applicant  has  been  employed  by  his  current  employer.   Between  February  2017  and  January  2018,  he  was  employed  by  another defense  contractor  as  a  director  of  electronic  systems  development.    He reported  unemployment  between  June  2016  and  February  2017.  Between  May  2006 2 and  May  2016,  Applicant  worked  for  a  non-defense  contractor  as  an  associate  vice president.  Citizenship and residency status of Applicant’s family members Applicant’s parents are both deceased and were citizens and residents of Russia before their deaths in 1968 and 1990, respectively. Applicant’s oldest daughter is a citizen of Israel who resides in Switzerland.  He maintains weekly contact with her and visits her once or twice a year. .
