Applicant admitted the two allegations in SOR: ¶¶ 1.a, and 1.b. After a thorough and  careful  review  of  the  pleadings,  exhibits,  and  testimony,  I  make  the  following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  31-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.   He has been employed with the defense contractor since July of 2016.   He  is  unmarried,  and  has  no  children.   Applicant attributes his admitted financial difficulties to periods of unemployment.  Guideline F – Financial Considerations 1.a.  Applicant  has  failed  to  file,  as  required,  Federal  income  tax  returns  for  tax years 2011~2013 and for 2016.  He attributes his failure to file  due  to  periods  of  overseas  deployment  while  in  the  Air  Force,  and to simple  procrastination.    Applicant  has  submitted documentation showing his filing for tax year 2014 , and he had no income in 2015 as he was unemployed . Despite having two months to do so, however, Applicant submitted nothing showing he has filed for tax years 2011~2013 and for 2016; and as such, this allegation is found against Applicant. 1.b. Applicant admits that he has a past-due debt to Creditor B in the amount of about $3,783.  Despite having  two  months  to  do  so,  Applicant  submitted  nothing  showing  he  has  addressed this admitted debt; and as such, this allegation is found against Applicant.
