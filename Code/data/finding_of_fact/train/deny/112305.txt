For tax purposes, abandonment is treated differently from foreclosure. Generally, abandonment is not treated as a sale or exchange of the property while a foreclosure is treated as a sale or exchange. If the amount realized from an abandonment is more than an individual’s adjusted basis, it amounts to a gain, if less, then it is a loss. See IRS Publication 544.  If property is abandoned that secures a debt for which one is personally liable  and the debt is cancelled, the individual realizes ordinary income equal to the canceled debt. That income must be reported on an individual’s tax return unless one of the exceptions or exclusions applies. See IRS Publication 4681.
