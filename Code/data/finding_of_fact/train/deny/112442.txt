In Applicant’s SOR response, he admitted the allegations in SOR ¶¶ 1.c, 1.d, 1.k, and 1.v.  Applicant’s admissions are accepted as findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  56-year-old  pilot  and  maintenance  specialist for  unmanned  aerial vehicles  and drones.   He  has  worked  for  the  same  government  contractor  since  July  2014.    He served in the Air Force for almost 28 years, and he honorably retired from the Air Force Reserve in 2008 as a master sergeant.  He served in Panama in Operation Just Cause,  in  Southwest  Asia  in  Desert  Shield/Desert  Storm,  in  Kosovo,  Iraq,  and Afghanistan.  His most recent deployments have been to Iraq and Africa.  2 In  1981,  Applicant  graduated  from  high  school,  and  in  2003,  he  received  a bachelor’s  degree  in  management.    In  1997,  he  married,  and  in  2014,  he divorced.  His children are ages 12 and 14.  Financial Considerations Applicant attributed his financial problems to his spouse’s failure to maintain his finances while he was deployed overseas.  She used illegal drugs.  She failed to make payments on two houses, and they were lost to foreclosure.  After they divorced in 2014, Applicant’s spouse did not pay her share of marital debts. Around the time of his divorce, he owed his brother $50,000 “and some other people.”  Applicant  was  deployed  in  2015  when  the  Internal  Revenue  Service    started garnishing  his  pay,  and that  is  when he first  learned he  had problems  with  his federal income taxes.  Applicant has been in the United States without being deployed overseas since March 2017.  He was injured during his last deployment.  His  current  annual  salary  is  about  $105,000.    He  has  custody  of  his  children; however, he does not receive any child support from his former spouse.  From May 2011 to May 2015, Applicant was not deployed, except for some brief trips overseas.  He was employed in fracking for almost two years in the United States.  He was mostly deployed from May 2015 to March 2017.
