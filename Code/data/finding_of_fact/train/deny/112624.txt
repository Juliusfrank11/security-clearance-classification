Applicant  has  been  employed  as  a master welder by a federal contractor since 2017. He reports no military service and he married in May 2016. Applicant has two daughters, age 3 months, and 10 years old. In section 22  of his Questionnaire for National Security Positions  or  security  clearance  application  ,  he  disclosed  an  arrest  for  driving  under  the influence    in  July  2012.  He  pled  guilty  to  a  reduced  charge  of  reckless  driving  in April  2013. He  completed probation and  community  service;  paid  fines and  costs;  and attended  mandatory,  court-ordered  DUI-counseling  sessions  one  day  each  week  from April to October 2013. He disclosed another arrest for DUI in November 2016. The SOR alleges that Applicant was also arrested for DUI and criminal mischief in  July  2005  .  Applicant  pled  no  contest  to  reduced  charges  of  reckless driving  and  criminal  mischief  in  October  2005,  and  he  was  sentenced  to  one  year probation  and  ordered  to  complete  a  DUI  level  one  program.  Applicant  admitted  all  of the  allegations  in  the  SOR  in  his  answer  to  the  SOR  in  July  2018.    He  also admitted  to  five  other  driving  offenses  including:  driving  while  license  suspended  in 2006, 2008, and 2017  and driving in violation of court-ordered license  restrictions  in  2007  and  2013  .  Additionally,  Applicant admitted being charged with possession of drug paraphernalia in January 2007.   State  court  records  provided  by  Department  Counsel  corroborate  Applicant’s admissions and confirm his multiple criminal offenses.  In his personal subject interview , Applicant stated that his latest November 2016 DUI occurred after he had dinner and three beers with friends and he was pulled over  by  police.  Applicant’s  blood  alcohol  content    was  .09  and  the  state  law threshold  for  DUI  is  .08  BAC.  Again,  he  paid  fines  and  $2,000  in  court  costs  plus  25 hours of community service, and one year probation. He completed mandatory alcohol abuse  counseling  in  January  2018,  and  his  license  was  revoked  for  one  year. Nonetheless,  Applicant  continued  to  drive  since  he  needed  to  get  to  work  and  felt  he had  no  other  choice.  In  2007,  when  he  was  charged  with  possession  of  marijuana,  it was  provided  to  him  at  a  party.
