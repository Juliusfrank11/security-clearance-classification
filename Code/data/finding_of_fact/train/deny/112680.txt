Applicant admitted to the one allegation of the SOR. After a thorough and careful review of the pleadings, exhibits, and testimony, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  37-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.   He has been employed with the defense contractor since 2004, and has held a security clearance since 2013.  Guideline G: Alcohol Consumption 1.a.  Applicant  started  drinking  alcohol  “regularly”  in  2006.    Until  2006,  his  regular  consumption  would  be  about  “eight  drinks”  at  one  sitting.   That  consumption  “increased  both  in frequency  and  in  quantity”  in  “about  2008.”    In  2013, Applicant was diagnosed as suffering from “fatty liver disease,” but Applicant continued to  consume  alcohol.    His  treating  physician  thought Applicant  was  drinking  excessively;  and  as  a  result,  “advised  .  .  .  [Applicant]  to  quit drinking.”  However, Applicant continued to consume alcohol. From September of 2013 until January of 2014, Applicant received treatment for his  liver  disease.    There  he  “learned  a  lot about  alcohol,  alcoholism,  or  alcohol  addiction.”    As  a  result,  Applicant  abstained from  alcohol  consumption  until  May  of  2014.    He  then returned to its consumption, “more than approximately six drinks at the time during the week. On the weekend . . . it was variable but would tend towards more 10 drinks at a time.”    In  October  of  2015,  and  again  in February of 2018, Applicant was diagnosed as suffering from an “alcohol use disorder, moderate.”    However,  Applicant  continued  to  consume  alcohol  until August of 2018, a month after the issuance of the SOR.  In the last seven months, Applicant has been committed to his alcohol abuse recovery, as evidenced by his authored progress reports.
