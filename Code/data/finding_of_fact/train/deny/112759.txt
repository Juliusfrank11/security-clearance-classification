Applicant  admitted  to  the  allegations  in  SOR  ¶¶  1.a.  through  1.g.,  and  1.k. through 1.n. He denied SOR allegations ¶¶ 1.h. through 1.j. After a thorough and careful review of the pleadings, exhibits, and testimony, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant is a 29-year-old employee of a defense contractor.   He  has  been  employed  with  the defense  contractor  since  March  of  2016,  but  he  does  not  currently  have  a  security clearance.  Applicant served in the Marine Corps from 2008~2013.   He  was  deployed  “to  Iraq  in  2009,” and “to Afghanistan in 2010 to 2011.”  As a result, he receives 70% compensation from  the  Veterans  Administration.    Applicant  received  an Honorable Discharge.  Guideline F - Financial Considerations Applicant  attributes  his  current  financial  difficulties  to  a  divorce  in  2012,  and periods of unemployment in 2013 and in 2014.  1.a.~1.d., 1.f., and 1.k.~1.n. Applicant admits that he has past-due debts to eight different creditors totaling about $20,121.   On New Year’s Eve, four months after the issuance of the SOR, Applicant sent the following two line, letter request to each of these creditors.  In each letter he simply requested the following: “this letter is being sent to you to request information on my account. I would like to be sent a statement with current amount due and minimum monthly  payment  options  to  be  able  to  resolve  this  credit  account.”    Applicant’s recent  requests,  though  laudable,  are  too  little  too  late.  He  has  had  years  to  address these  known  delinquencies  that  remain  outstanding.  These  allegations  are  found against Applicant. 1.e. Applicant has paid the alleged $35 past-due debt to Creditor E, as evidenced by correspondence with Creditor E.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 1.g. Applicant successfully disputed an alleged $14,257 past-due debt to Creditor G;  and  as  such,  it  does  not  appear  on  the  Government’s  two  most  recent  2018  credit reports.  This allegation is found for Applicant. 1.h.~1.j.  Applicant  successfully  disputed  three  alleged  past-due  debts,  totaling about $3,104, to Creditor H; and as such, they do not appears on the Government’s two 2 most  recent 2018  credit  reports.   These allegations are found for Applicant.
