In Applicant’s answer to the SOR, he admitted his home went to foreclosure, but denied the five additional delinquent obligations listed in the SOR. After a thorough review of the pleadings and exhibits, I make the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  49-year-old  program  manager  who  has  worked  for  a  defense contractor since February 2007 and seeks to obtain a security clearance. He is married, and  has  a  daughter  age  22  and  a  son  age  18.  His  annual  salary  is  approximately $125,000  and  his  wife,  a  special  education  school  teacher,  has  an  annual  salary  of $50,000.  She has been employed as a teacher for 22 years.  When  Applicant  completed  his  March  2017  Electronic  Questionnaires  for Investigation Processing , he indicated he had two delinquent obligations of $428 and $103 He also listed a delinquent $1,800 student loan and stated he was currently in the  process  of  establishing  a  monthly  $250  automatic  draft  to  pay  the  student  loan obligation.  The  SOR  alleges  six  delinquent debts,  and  the  record  establishes  the  status  of Applicant’s past-due accounts as follows: SOR ¶ 1.a alleges $27,084 was past due on his mortgage that went to foreclosure.  Applicant stated he purchased the home in 1997. He paid $72,000 for the home and obtained a mortgage requiring monthly mortgage payments of $1,200.  His July 2017 credit report indicating that in July 2006, the current lender obtained the 30- year mortgage with a balance of $76,000. The mortgage required monthly payments of $993.  As of July 2017, the balance owed was $78,516. In  June  2018,  Applicant  had  an  enhanced  subject  interview  during  which  his finances  were  discussed.  His  wife  failed  to  pay  the  mortgage  for  four  or  five  months resulting in $27,084 becoming past due.  He was unaware his wife was not making the  mortgage  payments  as  required.  He  indicated  that  his  wife  was  handling  the 2 household’s  finances.    That  has  since  changed,  Applicant  has  taken  a more active role in the household’s finances. Applicant went to court and was given the option to bring the mortgage current or vacate the property.  He chose to leave. The bank gave him three months to leave.  In August 2016, the bank forced him to leave the residence.  He asserts he has not received any correspondence from the lender following the foreclosure nor has he contacted the lender since leaving the home.  He does not know the home’s sale price at foreclosure nor the amount of equity he accumulated during the 19 years he owned the home.  He says failing to list the foreclosure on his March 2017 was not an intentional omission or misrepresentation.  SOR ¶ 1.b alleges an $834 charged-off credit card account.  The account was  opened  in  July  2017.    In  his  December  2018  SOR  Response  and  at  the hearing, Applicant stated he did not know the nature of the debt.  SOR ¶ 1.c alleges a $1,356 charged-off account.  The account was opened in  January  2017.    In  his  December  2018  SOR  Response  and  at  the  hearing, Applicant did not recognize this debt.  SOR  ¶  1.d  alleges  a  $428  collection  account.    During  his  June  2018 interview, Applicant indicated the debt was a credit card account. He had no knowledge about why it was not paid or when it went to collection. He asserts he paid the debt, but provided no documentation supporting such payment.  In his December 2018 SOR Response and at the hearing, Applicant indicated he did not recognize this debt.  SOR ¶ 1.e alleges a $415 charged-off account.  During his June 2018 interview, Applicant indicated he still has a credit card with this company, and would follow up on the debt and satisfy it.  SOR ¶ 1.f alleges a $103 telephone service collection account.  During his June 2018 interview, Applicant indicated he was unaware of this debt. He contacted the creditor and was told this was an old land-line account. He stated he believed this debt was paid when the land line was disconnected. He provided no documentation showing payment of the debt. He indicated he would follow up on the debt and satisfy it.  In Applicant’s December 2018 SOR Response, for the delinquent obligations listed in SOR 1.b through 1.f., he stated. “I will work with this company to pay what is owed to them. I am not aware of this company.”  At the hearing, he recognized the debts in SOR 1.a and 1.f. He said he would contact the companies and take care of the  debts.    He  provided  no  documentation  that  he  made  any  payments  on  the SOR delinquent obligations during the four months between his receipt of the SOR and the hearing. There is no showing he has contacted any of the holders of the delinquent obligations.  At his hearing, he stated he would contact the companies and pay the debts within two months of the hearing.  3 Applicant has been renting a home for three years and has always been timely on his $1,100 monthly rent payments.  He has $30,000 in his 401 retirement plan.  He is current on his wife’s, his daughter’s, and son’s vehicle payments that total $1,300 per month.  Applicant has not received any financial counseling.   He  is  very  involved  in  the  community.    He  volunteers  for  the  Special Olympics and has helped to raise $300,000 for the organization over the years.  He is also involved in a drug-free city organization, the county Relay for Life, and is very involved in civic organizations, being the past president of one.  The record was kept open to allow Applicant to submit any documentation showing payment of the delinquent obligations or any other material he believed was relevant.  No documents were received.
