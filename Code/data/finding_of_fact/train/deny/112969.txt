Financial Considerations Applicant’s  SOR  in  ¶¶  1.d,  1.c,  1.b,  and  1.a  alleges he filed  bankruptcy  in 2001, 2010,  2017,  and  2018.  Applicant’s  Office  of  Personnel  Management    personal subject interview  took place on January 23, 2018.  The bankruptcies will be discussed in chronological order. SOR  ¶  1.d  alleges  Applicant  filed  for  Chapter  7  bankruptcy  in  August  2001.  His nonpriority unsecured debts were discharged in November 2001. Applicant said his first spouse was committing adultery, spending excessively, and gambling.  He blamed his first spouse for mishandling the family finances while he was away from home for training.  His debts totaled $23,600.  Applicant agreed to take responsibility for the family debts in the divorce; Applicant obtained custody of his son; and his spouse agreed to pay $150 monthly in child support.  He believed after the divorce and his bankruptcy the creditors did not seek payment from his former spouse of their debts.  Child care was expensive.  SOR ¶ 1.c alleges Applicant filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy in May 2010, and he claimed $56,368 in nonpriority unsecured claims. His nonpriority unsecured debts were discharged in October 2010. After Applicant retired from the Air Force in 2007, his income was reduced because he was underemployed.  His Air Force monthly base pay was $4,142 .   He also received $1,300 in housing allowance and $637 for rations and special pay.  His gross pay on active duty was about $73,000. He said he also had a period of unemployment.  In 2007 and 2008, he earned about $52,000 to $54,000 annually.  In 2009, his gross income was about $58,000 .   Before  filing  for  bankruptcy,  he  said  he  attempted  to  use  a  debt  consolidation  and payment plan  to resolve his debts.  He terminated his participation in the DCPP because the DCPP used most of his payments for service charges, and Applicant decided DCPP was not making sufficient progress resolving his debts.  SOR ¶ 1.b alleges Applicant filed for Chapter 13 bankruptcy in May 2017. In June 2018,  this  bankruptcy  was  dismissed  for  failure  to  make  plan  payments.  In  2015,  his annual income was about $74,000 .  In 2016, Applicant’s gross income was about $10,000 less  and  totaled  $64,000  .
