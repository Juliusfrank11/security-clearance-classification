In  Applicant’s SOR response, he  admitted  the  SOR allegations  in ¶¶  1.a  through 1.j  with  explanations.   Applicant’s admissions  are accepted  as  findings of fact.  Additional findings follow. Applicant is 62 years old, and he is seeking employment as a translator.   In  1980,  he  graduated  from  high  school,  and  in  1984,  he  was  awarded  a bachelor’s degree.  He has not served in the military.  He served in Iraq as a translator from 2010 to 2011, and he held a security clearance at that time.  He was unemployed for about one year after returning from Iraq.   In 1999, he married, and in 2018, he divorced.  His four children were born in 2002, 2004, 2006, and 2014.  There is no evidence that Applicant violated his employer’s rules, committed criminal conduct, used illegal drugs, or abused alcohol.  Financial Considerations Applicant attributed his delinquent debts to his  divorce, and his decision to take responsibility for the marital debts.  He was underemployed and lacked sufficient income  to  pay  his  debts.    About  four  months  before  his  hearing,  he  sought professional  help  from  a  financial  advisor  with  his  debts.    From  July  through August 2021, he was hospitalized on three occasions.  He was unable to work for four months.  He  has been  working part time as  a  translator since 2015.    He  also  delivers  food  for  restaurants.    He  is  currently receiving Social Security payments.  He does not have any savings or retirement accounts.  SOR ¶¶ 1.a through 1.j allege that Applicant had 10 delinquent accounts totaling $85,859 for the following amounts: $25,651; $24,161; $16,858; $5,545; $3,495; $2,851; $2,517; $478; $335; and $3,968.  For 4 of the 10 debts, the creditors attempted to garnish Applicant’s pay; however, Applicant did not know the result of the lawsuits.  Applicant stopped paying the SOR debts in 2017, except for SOR ¶ 1.b .  The debts were generated for living expenses at a time when Applicant was unemployed.  Due to lack of income, he did not make any payments on the other SOR debts.  He sought professional help with his debts from a financial advisor.  His financial advisor is supposed to  contact his creditors and discuss the debts with his creditors.  He does not have a plan for resolving his SOR debts.  He has given information to his financial advisor; however, he has not signed a contract with the financial advisor for services.  He is relying on the financial advisor for help and advice about what he should do about his debts.   He has not received financial counseling.  As for the debt in SOR ¶ 1.b for $24,161 for child support, Applicant explained that he  provides  some  financial  support  to  his  family.    He  has  receipts  for  the 3 payments he made to support his children.  He purchased used cars for two of his daughters in 2017 and 2019.  He has a lawyer who is helping with the child support debt.  He said he is current on his child support debt.  I requested that Applicant provide his federal income tax returns for the last five years to show his income.  I requested that he provide documentation showing his  history  of  making  child  support  payments,  and  documentation  concerning  his agreement with the professional financial advisor.  He did not provide any post-hearing documentation.
