In Applicant’s SOR response, he admitted all of the SOR allegations.  He also provided mitigating information.  His admissions are accepted as findings of fact. Applicant is a 57-year-old engineering technician II who has worked for a defense contractor for three years.  In 1984, he graduated from high school.  He has never served in the military.  He was married in 1987 and divorced in 1991.  His two children were born in 1987 and 2004.  Financial Considerations For several years, Applicant was paid cash for labor work without the information being shared with the Internal Revenue Service .  He has a history of not filing tax returns because his refunds were going toward a child support debt, and he did not believe this was fair because he was not permitted to see his daughter.   His  child  support  debt  for  his  oldest  child  was  paid  in  2016.    He acknowledge that his handling of his taxes was not “the right thing to do.”  Later, after  his  child  support  was  paid,  he  failed  to  file  some  tax  returns  because  he  was distracted  by  other  events  and  issues  in  his  life,  including  the  illness  and  death  of  his father.  SOR ¶ 1.a alleges Applicant failed to file his federal income tax returns for TYs 2016 and 2018. Applicant said he was unable to find W-2s or other documents showing his income for TY 2016.  For TY 2016, he provided an IRS account transcript showing no tax return was found.
