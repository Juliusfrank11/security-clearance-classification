Personal Conduct SOR ¶ 2.a alleges Applicant failed to disclose on his February 13, 2020 SCA the information in SOR ¶¶ 1.a through 1.i concerning his delinquent debts. Applicant did not disclose any debts placed for collection or charged off in the previous seven years on his February 13, 2020 SCA.  Applicant said he completed an SCA in 2017, and then he changed employment.  In 2020, he was supposed to complete his SCA because his 2017 SCA was apparently not processed.  For his 2020 SCA, he updated the employment history; however, he hurried through the  remainder of the SCA and did not read the questions 4 carefully.    He completed   the  SCA  as  quickly   as  he   could.     He  was  just checking off the  no  blocks without really reading the questions.  He compared it to  completion  of  a  medical questionnaire  when  one  has an  appointment with  a  physician. He did not disclose  his  foreclosure or the  other delinquent debts  on  his SOR.   He  was  “complacent”  about   his  SCA.    He  did  not  intend   to   deceive   the government.   He held  a  security  clearance  for 17  years, and  he  did not believe  his SCA or background raised any  security issues.
