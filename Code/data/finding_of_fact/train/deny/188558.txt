After a thorough  and  careful  review  of  the  pleadings,  exhibits,  and  testimony,  I  make  the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  37-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.  He  has  been employed  with  the defense  contractor since February of  2019.  Applicant  was divorced from  his  first  wife  in  2012  and  remarried  in  2018.  He  has  two  children  from  his  first marriage.  He attributes much of his admitted past-due debt to his decade old divorce.  Guideline F - Financial Considerations 1.p.   Applicant  filed  for  the  protection  of  a  Chapter  7  bankruptcy  in  August  of 2009, and his debts were discharged in January of 2010. 1.a.  Applicant  admits  that  he  is  indebted  to State  C for delinquent  taxes  for  tax year 2016, in the amount of about $1,119.  He has offered nothing  further in this regard; and as such, this allegation is found against Applicant. 1.b.  and   1.c.   Applicant  admits  that  he  has  failed  to  file  his  Federal  and  state income  taxes  for tax  years 2017~2020.    He has offered documentation showing that he has filed his income tax returns for tax year 2021.    These  allegations,  as  to  tax  years  2017~2020,  are  found  against Applicant. 1.d.~1.o.   Applicant  admits  that  he  has  past-due  debts  to  twelve  separate creditors  totaling  about  $44,008.    He  has offered nothing further in this regard. These allegations are found against Applicant.
