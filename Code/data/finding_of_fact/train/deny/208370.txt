After a  thorough  and  careful  review  of  the  pleadings,  exhibits,  and  testimony,  I  make  the following findings of fact. Applicant  is  a  35-year-old  employee  of  a  defense  contractor.  He  has  been employed with the defense contractor since February of 2022. He is divorced, and has one child.  Applicant blames his current financial situation a period of unemployment, COVID-19, making “some bad financial decisions,” and “living without . . . [his] means.”  Guideline F - Financial Considerations 1.a. Applicant filed for the protection of a Chapter 7 bankruptcy in June of 2018; and as a result, his debts at that time were discharged in September of 2018.   Since that  discharge,  Applicant  has  accumulated  additional  past-due indebtedness, listed below. 1.b.~1.d.  and   1.f.  Applicant  admits  past-due  debts  to  four  different  creditors totaling  about  $19,835.    Applicant’s  plan  of action  was  to  take  no  affirmative  action,  but  to  wait  for  his  creditors  to  “reach  out”  to Applicant.    He  was informed  that this lack  of  action was not a good-faith effort to address his past-due indebtedness  Applicant has submitted nothing further in this regard. These past-due debts are found against Applicant. 1.e. Applicant denies a past-due debt to Creditor E in the amount of $87.  As this debt still appears as “outstanding” on one of the Government’s March  2022 credit reports , this allegation is found against Applicant.
