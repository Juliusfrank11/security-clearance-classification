Applicant is a 37-year-old employee of a defense contractor. He has worked for
his current employer since May 2018. He is applying for a security clearance for the first
time.  He  has  associate’s,  bachelor’s,  and  master’s  degrees,  which  were  awarded  in
2003, 2006, and 2010. He is married without children. (Transcript (Tr.) at 17, 23-24; GE
1, 2)
The  SOR  alleges  four  defaulted  student  loans  with  balances  totaling  $37,522.
Applicant admitted owing the loans, but he stated that he is paying them.
Applicant funded his education with student loans. He is current on a number of
loans  that  were  not  alleged  in  the  SOR.  The  four  SOR  loans  were  taken  out  between
2003 and 2005 from the same bank. Applicant provided his payment history for the four
loans.  He  made  payments  from  2007  through  2009,  but  then  no  additional  payments
until 2013. (Tr. at 20-21; Applicant’s response to SOR; GE 2-4; AE A)
Applicant made five payments to the loans in 2013; seven payments in 2014; five
payments  in  2015; and four payments  in  2016. With  the  exception  of  a few  months  in
which  there  were  no  payments,  he  has  consistently  made  monthly  payments  to  the
bank  since  October  2017.  The  bank  distributes  the  funds  between  the  four  loans.  He
increased  the  amount  of  his  payments  to  $200  per  month  in  July  2018  and  $250  per
month  in  June  2019.  His  payments  from  October  2017  through  August  2019  total
$2,860. He credibly testified that he intends to continue paying all his student loans. (Tr.
at 17-24; Applicant’s response to SOR; GE 2-4; AE A)
Applicant  submitted  letters  attesting  to  his  excellent  job  performance.  He  is
praised  for  his  work  ethic,  trustworthiness,  dedication,  professionalism,  dependability,
and integrity. (AE B)
