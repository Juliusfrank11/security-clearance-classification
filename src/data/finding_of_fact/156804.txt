Guideline H - Drug Involvement and Substance Misuse
The Government alleges that the Applicant has used controlled substances that
cause  physical  or  mental  impairment  or  are  used  in  a  manner  inconsistent  with  their
intended  purpose,  which  can  raise  questions  about  an  individual’s  reliability  and
trustworthiness.
Applicant admits the single allegation under this guideline.  (Government Exhibit
1.)  He has used marijuana off and on from approximately 2014 to at least March 2020.
Applicant began using marijuana in his senior year of high school, and smoked it five or
six times total.  In college his use of marijuana increased to five times weekly.  In 1989,
he focused on family obligations and raising his children, and he did not use marijuana
again until 2014.  From 2014 to the present, Applicant has used marijuana weekly with
friends  at  their  homes,  social  events  or  gatherings.  He  has  also  used  it  at  his  own
residence on weekends.  Applicant continues to use marijuana.  He now uses marijuana
once  or  twice  a  week.  Applicant  stated  that  he  uses  marijuana  because  he  finds  it
relaxing and fun.  He obtains it by purchasing it.  Applicant claims that he has told his
supervisor that he uses marijuana and his supervisor did not comment.  Applicant does
not know if his employer has a drug policy.  (Government Exhibit 3.)
Applicant  began  working  for  his  current  employer  in  September  2014.  On
January 29, 2020, Applicant completed a security clearance application.  (Government
Exhibit 2.)  After completing this application, Applicant has continued to use marijuana.
(Government Exhibit 1.)  Applicant intends to continue smoking marijuana in the future.
