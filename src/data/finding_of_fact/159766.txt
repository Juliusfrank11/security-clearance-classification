Applicant is a 30-year-old employee of a defense contractor, where she works as
an associate software developer. She has a Bachelor’s degree. She is seeking to obtain
national security eligibility for a security clearance in connection with her employment in
the Defense industry. (Government Exhibit 1 at Sections12 and 13A.)
Paragraph  1 (Guideline H –  Drug Involvement  and Substance Misuse)
The Government alleges in this paragraph that Applicant is ineligible for clearance
due  to  her  use  of  illegal  drugs.  Applicant  admitted  allegations  1.a  and  1.b  under  this
paragraph.
1.a.  Applicant  has  used  marijuana  on  a  daily  basis,  with  some  breaks,  since
approximately 2014.  Applicant  stated  that  she  used marijuana  in  an  attempt  to  relieve
work-related  stress.  She  stated  in  her  Answer,  “Marijuana  allows  me  to  manage  my
anxiety without hurting my ability to do good work.” (Item 3 at 4.)
1.b. Applicant stated that she intended to continue to use marijuana at the same
level.
Applicant stated that her marijuana use is responsible. She sees a therapist and
has a state-issued medical marijuana card. Applicant argued that the Federal proscription
on marijuana use is outdated. (Answer; Applicant Exhibit A.)
MITIGATION
Appellant submitted her resume and her most recent performance review. She is
a talented software developer. Her manager stated, “[Applicant] has proven herself to be
a  smart,  capable,  team  player  and  hard  worker.”  The  manager  further  stated  that
Applicant, “has exceeded expectations in tasks and deliverables she has worked on.”
