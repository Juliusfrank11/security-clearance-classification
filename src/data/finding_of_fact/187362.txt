In  Applicant’s  answer  to  the  SOR,  she  admitted  all  the  allegations.  Her
admissions are incorporated in my findings of fact.
Applicant is a 33-year-old employee of a federal contractor. She has taken online
college  courses  but  does  not  have  a  degree.  She  has  never  married  and  has  no
children. She has never held a security clearance.
The SOR alleges a Chapter 7 bankruptcy (SOR ¶ 1.a) and 19 delinquent debts
totaling  about $14,741.  All the debts alleged in  the SOR are  reflected  in  credit  reports
from  June  2022  and  October  2020  (FORM  Items  5  and  6).  Applicant  admitted  all  the
debts in her answer to the SOR, but provided no information explaining how they were
incurred or what actions, if any, she had taken to resolve them.
When  Applicant  submitted  her  SCA,  she  disclosed  that  she  filed  a  Chapter  7
bankruptcy  petition  in  February  2016  and  received  a  discharge  in  May  2016.  Debts
totaling  about  $10,000  were  discharged.  (FORM  Item  3  at  33.)  In  the  same  SCA,  she
also  disclosed  the  debts  alleged  in  SOR  ¶¶  1.d,  1.e,  1.g,  and  1.h,  attributed  them  to
“financial  difficulties,”  and  stated  that  she  had  asked  to  settle  them  through  an  online
service. (FORM Item 1 at 35-37)
In  her  answer  to  DOHA  interrogatories  in  March  2022,  Applicant  admitted  the
debts alleged in SOR ¶¶ 1.b-1.m, 1.p, and 1.r. For each debt, she admitted that the debt
was not paid, and no arrangements had been made to pay it. (FORM Item 4 at 2-6)
