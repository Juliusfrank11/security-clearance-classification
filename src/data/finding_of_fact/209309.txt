Applicant, age 61, is an employee of DOD contractor who is seeking to maintain
his security clearance. He has worked for the same employer since 2001. He served on
active duty in United States Air Force from 1981 to 2001. He retired with an Honorable
discharge.  He  believes  he  has  held  a  security  clearance  consistently  since  1981.  He
earned an associate’s degree in 1996. He married in 1980 and divorced in 2019, He has
an adult daughter. (Item 2)
The   SOR  alleged   nine   delinquent  debts,  with   a   combined   approximate   total  of
$120,120,  to  include:   a  $40,281  charged-off  account  (SOR ¶  1.a:  Item  4  at  5; Item  3  at
2);  a   $32,071   charged-off   account   (SOR  ¶   1.b:  Item   4   at  3;   Item   3   at  2);  a   $19,242
charged-off  credit  card  account  (SOR ¶  1.c:  Item  4  at 4; Item  3  at 2);  a  $16,919  charged-
off  credit card account (SOR ¶  1.d:  Item  4  at  4; Item  3  at  3);  a  $5,505  charged-off  credit
card  account   (SOR  ¶   1.e:  Item   4   at  7;  Item   3   at  3);  a   $4,408   charged-off   credit  card
account (SOR ¶  1.f: Item  4  at 6; Item  3  at 3); a  delinquent telephone  account in the  amount
of $1,076  placed  for collection  (SOR ¶  1.g: Item  3  at 4);  a  $318  satellite  television  account
placed  for collection  (SOR ¶  1.h: Item  4  at 4; Item  3  at 4);  and  a  $300  cable television
account placed  for collection  (SOR ¶  1.i: Item 3 at 4).
Applicant indicated in Section 26 - Financial Record of his February 2022 SCA that
his  debts  became  delinquent  because  he  earned  significantly  less  income  and  his
financial situation was complicated by his 2019 divorce. He hopes to improve his income
so he can satisfy his debts. (Item 2 at 45-54)
The SOR also alleges Applicant failed to file his federal income tax returns for tax
years 2018 and 2019. (SOR ¶ 1.j: Item 2 at 46) He failed to file his state income tax returns
for tax years 2018 and 2019. . (SOR ¶ 1.k: Item 2 at 46) Applicant listed his failure to file
federal and state income tax returns in response to Section 26 Financial Record – Taxes
on his February 2022 SCA.  Regarding tax year 2018, he commented that he expected a
tax refund and just forgot to file. He indicated the filing of his 2019 federal and state tax
returns were delayed because of COVID 19 filing extensions. He noted that he expected
a refund and payroll withholding was active throughout the year. (Item 2 at 46)
In his response to the SOR, Applicant admits to all of the allegations in the SOR.
He states under each SOR allegation:
This  is  a  self-reported  item,  but  it  by  no  means  indicates  unreliability,
untrustworthiness, or my ability to protect classified information. In fact, the
opposite  is  true  by  readily  admitting  to  the  government  that  these  issues
exist. It also by no means indicates that I have, or will in the future, engage
in any illegal activities, or otherwise questionable acts (definition of which is
lacking substance and is subjective. (Item 1)
Applicant did not provide evidence indicating that he is attempting to pay or resolve
any of the delinquent debts alleged in the SOR. He also did not provide proof that he filed
his federal and state income taxes for 2018 and 2019. His approximate monthly income
is $7,244, His monthly expenses total $7,177. His total net remainder each month is $67.
(Item  5  at  6)  Based  on  his  monthly  budget,  he  is  unable  to  resolve  his  delinquent
accounts.
